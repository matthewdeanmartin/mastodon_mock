About Mawkingbird

Why I asked a bot to create this

I miss 2018 twitter. It was before the redesign that still is on the renamed twitter. So goal one is to bring back the proper design. You use to be able to do that with a browser plugin, but eventually Twitter broke that. This first of all, fixes that.

Other things this client has

  • Twitter Blue features, but for free. It was only a few features, but I didn’t like that those features were held back. I don’t know what it would have taken to get me to give twitter money. They should have monetized businesses.
  • You can give everyone a blue check, or a check to everyone with more follower than you
  • No infinite feed. It will eventually stop. You have to click to get to the next page. Your behavioral additions do not profit me, why should my free client keep you from friends and family and touching grass?
  • Bookmarks tacked on to end of the finite feed. All clients give too little attention to the bookmark workflow. Bookmarks are to be revisited.
  • The algorithm isn’t evil, meaning no injecting rage or strangers content that I didn’t ask for. It is basically your tags and feed but sorted by the product of likes, replies and retweets
  • Not trying to be TweetDeck. I never got used to tweetdeck, a product can’t be all things to all people.
  • Failwhale is back. Failwhale was a cultural icon and Twitter decided to take that good will and shit it out because, I don’t know, some PM probably didn’t like that it brought attention to operational failures at twitter.
  • You can follow everyone on a collection. Because the APIs are there and no one can stop me.
  • House ads. I want to promote my other projects. I don’t see a way to monetize this, but I hope to monetize a hosted freemium/premium client called “Mastodon is my Blog” which is a much more ambitious project that doesn’t work great until you have a 1GB cache. I don't have cyber begging links for myself because I don't have an audience that lends itself to cyberbegging.
  • I added cyber begging links for the Mastodon network and your instance. This stuff isn’t free, go help them out.
  • Pretty much all Mastodon features are supported or will be soon.
  • Bsky integration as a feed provider. Friends I made over a period of a decade went to Bsky instead of Mastodon. Bsky isn’t evil, I have no incentive to make them switch to Mastodon.
  • RSS as a provider has basic support, I want to expand it.
  • I miss Twitter Analytics. This version has to work of just a little bit of data and we have very little info about friends and follows and views. So it’s only similar.
  • I don't like social media abusing my attention, so infinite feed is replace with a "load more button". The chat and notifications don't light up when a chat arrives. Check your inbox on your schedule.
  • Finding accounts to follow is harder than it needs to be, in part because the default search is missing modern features, like saved searches, facets, and so on.
  • For a privacy focused community, clients sure do fail to give you a logged in experience. This is surprising because the APIs let you do a lot without logging in.

Why is it called mawkingbird? Because mockingbird as a domain is taken. I started this project while working on a mock mastodon server to be used for integration testing my other mastodon projects.

Implementation

It is written in Angular.
Uses latest signals patterns
Tested against a mock mastodon instance and real instances.
I’ve been using it as my daily driver and I now like it better than my previous favorite, elk.zone which I still recommend over modern day twitter or the default mastodon client.
