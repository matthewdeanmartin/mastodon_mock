Follow your people wherever they post. Mastodon is home — connections are merged into your home timeline as guests, and everything here stays in this browser (localStorage), like all Mockingbird preferences.

📡 RSS feeds

Any RSS or Atom feed becomes posts in your home feed, read-only, with an "Open original" link. Mockingbird has no server, so a feed only works if its site allows browser (CORS) access — you'll know right away when you add it.

Up to 10 feeds can be saved in this browser.

💧 Raindrop.io bookmarks

Add Raindrop.io as a second bookmark provider. When you bookmark a post, you can save the post itself or unwrap it and save its first external link. Hashtags and links back to your Mastodon instance are skipped.

  1. Create an app in Raindrop.io's App Management Console.
  2. Set its OAuth redirect URI to the exact callback shown below. The address is specific to this deployment: local /_ui/, canary /canary/, and production each have a different callback.
  3. Paste that app's client ID and client secret below, then choose “Save and connect”.
{{ raindropRedirectUrl }}

Raindrop.io requires an exact match. If you test another deployment, register its displayed callback there and use credentials for that app.

Your client ID, client secret, and OAuth tokens are stored in this browser's localStorage. Only use this on a device and browser profile you trust.
